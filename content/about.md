# Dora Helen Impson

"Posterity! You will never know how much it cost the present generation to preserve your freedom. I hope you will make good use of it." These words from John Adams articulate why I have chosen to become a historian.

My love of history stems from my grandparents, who were part of the generation that endured World War II. They were my playmates and first history teachers, and I owe them an outstanding debt. My thirst for historical knowledge is rooted in the lessons I learned at my grandparents' side. The importance of sharing this knowledge with future generations was impressed on me by those who truly understood the price that has been paid for freedom.

I am a Ph.D student studying Digital History at Clemson where my research interests include looking at the intersection of racial relations, religious beliefs, and civil rights in American History through the lens of Legal History. Topics of interest to me are: the Black experience in America in terms of property ownership and loss, lament and the American church's responses to racial atrocities, and the use of Christianity as agency in American history.

I am interested in partnering with other digital historians to explore how to make history from the Reconstruction to modern times accessible, engaging, and inspiring for young people in the United States.
